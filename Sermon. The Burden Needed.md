1. Habakkuk
	1. A prophet contemporary with Jeremiah
	2. He lived in Judia during the reign of King Jehoiakim.
		1. This is the King that took God's Word, cut it up, and burned it in the fire.
		2. He wanted nothing to do with God and His Word. (detailed in Jeremiah)
		3. God has said that the leader is a mirror of the people.
			- 1 Samuel 12:14 "If ye will fear the LORD, and serve him, and obey his voice, and not rebel against the commandment of the LORD, then shall both ye and also the king that reigneth over you continue following the LORD your God:"
			- 1ซามูเอล 12:14 "ถ้าท่านทั้งหลายจะยำเกรงพระเยโฮวาห์ และปรนนิบัติพระองค์ และเชื่อฟังพระสุรเสียงของพระองค์ และไม่กบฏต่อพระบัญชาของพระเยโฮวาห์ ท่านทั้งหลายและกษัตริย์ผู้ปกครองเหนือท่านจึงจะเป็นผู้ติดตามพระเยโฮวาห์พระเจ้าของท่านทั้งหลายต่อไป"
				1. If they listen to and follow God, so will their King.
		4. Since their King wants nothing to do with God, neither does the people in Israel.
2. A burden (ภาระในใจของเรา) leads to action
	- Habakkuk 1:1 "The burden which Habakkuk the prophet did see."
	- ฮาบากุก 1:1 "ภาระที่ฮาบากุกผู้พยากรณ์ได้เห็นมา"
		1. Habakkuk had a deep burden for the state of Israel
			1. No one wanted to follow God
			2. Everyone just did what they wanted and never gave thought to God.
		2. Churches don't grow and people don’t trust in God when they don't have a burden.
			1. When you think everything is just fine, you don’t do anything.
			2. When you realize that there is a problem, you get a burden to solve it.
		3. What Habakkuk saw burdened him.
			1. People not knowing God.
			2. People not following God.
			3. People just simply don't care about God.
		4. Does this burden you?
3. A burdened heart cries out to God
	- Habakkuk 1:2 "O LORD, how long shall I cry, and thou wilt not hear! even cry out unto thee of violence, and thou wilt not save!"
	- ฮาบากุก 1:2 "โอ ข้าแต่พระเยโฮวาห์ ข้าพระองค์จะร้องทุกข์นานสักเท่าใด และพระองค์จะมิได้ทรงฟังหรือ ข้าพระองค์จะร้องทูลต่อพระองค์เรื่องความทารุณ และพระองค์ก็จะไม่ทรงช่วยให้รอด"
		1. This isn't a despairing cry.
		2. It is a cry for help with the current situation.
			1. Like when a mother sees her child in danger and cries out for help.
		3. What he saw around him made him cry to God.
			1. He was attentive to the people around him and their danger.
			2. We don't help others until we understand their danger.
				1. When we don't see a need to get involved, we don’t get involved for fear of getting hurt.
				2. But when we have a burden, like a someone rescuing a child from a fire, we will not think of self but for the others in need.
		4. Just like Jeremiah
			- Lamentations 3:51 "Mine eye affecteth mine heart because of all the daughters of my city."
			- เพลงคร่ำครวญ 3:51 "นัยน์ตาของข้าพระองค์ทำให้ใจข้าพระองค์ระทมเพราะเหตุบรรดาบุตรสาวแห่งกรุงข้าพระองค์"
				1. They both saw the condition of those around them:
					1. Lost
					2. Heading for a fierce judgement
				2. Do we see those around us like that?
					1. If so, why are we not burdened for them?
					2. Do we not love them? 
						1. Those we love the most we are willing the help the most.
					3. God calls us to love our neighbors as ourselves.
						1. You would want someone to tell you the Gospel.
						2. We should tell them.
4. No matter what, the man walking with God rejoices in God.
	- Habakkuk 3:17-18 "Although the fig tree shall not blossom, neither shall fruit be in the vines; the labour of the olive shall fail, and the fields shall yield no meat; the flock shall be cut off from the fold, and there shall be no herd in the stalls: Yet I will rejoice in the LORD, I will joy in the God of my salvation."
	- ฮาบากุก 3:17-18 "แม้ต้นมะเดื่อจะไม่มีดอกบาน หรือจะไม่มีผลในเถาองุ่น การตรากตรำกับต้นมะกอกเทศก็สูญเปล่า ทุ่งนาจะมิได้เกิดอาหาร ฝูงสัตว์จะขาดไปจากคอก และจะไม่มีฝูงวัวที่ในโรงนา ถึงกระนั้นข้าพเจ้าจะร่าเริงในพระเยโฮวาห์ ข้าพเจ้าจะเปรมปรีดิ์ในพระเจ้าแห่งความรอดของข้าพเจ้า"
		1. Habakkuk knew that if the people didn't change that God would have to judge.
			1. Judgement brings people to the end of themselves.
			2. Most people will not seek for God until they hit rock bottom.
			3. The judgement is for eliminating those that will never change.
		2. Even though God brings judgment, Habakkuk rejoiced in God!
			1. Nothing shakes a person walking with God.
			2. He knew what is going to happen and trusted fully in God protection and help.
		3. The person walking with God knows that God will take care of them even when things look bleak.
			1. Jeremiah lived through the destruction without a problem.
			2. Nothing is recorded about what happened to Habakkuk.
