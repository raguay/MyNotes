Isaiah 40:28-31

1. God never gets tired
   1. He is patient and kind
   2. No news or circumstance can cause Jesus to be faint, willing to give up - v. 28
   3. He impowers us to follow after Jesus - v. 29
2. Everyone should wait Upon God
   1. The lost
      1. They will faint and be weary - v. 30
      2. Man can't seek after God on their own
         - Romans 3:11 'There is none that understandeth, there is none that seeketh after God.'
         - โรม 3:11 "ไม่มีคนที่เข้าใจ ไม่มีคนที่แสวงหาพระเจ้า"
           1. All mankind aren't interested in a boss
           2. They want to control their own lives
      3. God has to work in man's heart to know him
         - Philippians 2:13: 'For it is God which worketh in you both to will and to do of his good pleasure.'
         - ฟิลิปี 2:13 'เพราะว่าพระเจ้าเป็นผู้ทรงกระทำกิจอยู่ภายในท่าน ทั้งให้ท่านมีใจปรารถนาและให้ประพฤติตามชอบพระทัยของพระองค์'
      4. Therefore, they have to wait on God to move their hearts to seek after Him.
         1. No one is in church by their own wills
         2. It is God working in their hearts to know Him
         3. But, they then need to act upon it and accept Him
            - Genesis 6:3 'And the LORD said, My spirit shall not always strive with man, for that he also is flesh...'
            - ปฐมกาล 6:3 "คำเตือนของพระเจ้าถึงการพิพากษาพระเยโฮวาห์ตรัสว่า "วิญญาณของเราจะไม่วิงวอนกับมนุษย์ตลอดไป เพราะเขาเป็นแต่เนื้อหนัง..."
              1. God only works on a heart for a time peiod
              2. No one know how long that will be
      5. The saved need to pray asking God to work in their hearts.
         1. God works harder on those we pray for Him to save
   2. The saved - v. 31
      1. Doesn't mean inactivity
         1. Laziness will never achieve the work of God
            - 2 Thessalonians 3:10 'For even when we were with you, this we commanded you, that if any would not work, neither should he eat.'
            - 2เธสะโลนิกา 3:10 "แม้เมื่อเราอยู่กับพวกท่าน เราก็ได้กำชับท่านอย่างนี้ว่า ถ้าผู้ใดไม่ยอมทำงาน ก็อย่าให้เขากิน"
         2. This promise is for those that diligently seek after Him
      2. It means to keep going with
         1. The basics of Christian living (home work)
            - read bible, prayer, church attendence, witnessing
         2. last orders received (special jobs)
            - continue working towards Gods goals while waiting on God
      3. But, we have to be willing to follow and trust after God
         1. What would make you not wait upon the Lord?
         2. It is a good way to measure our closeness to God.
         3. Not close to God
            1. unaswered prayer
            2. Things not going the way we want
            3. Complaining in anyway shows a lackness in drawing close to God.
         4. Close to God
            1. Nothing would cause you not to wait upon the Lord
            2. Daniel trusted God as he fell into the lions den
            3. Shadrack, Mesheck, and Abednigo was thrown into the fire while waiting for God.
3. Benifits of Waiting - v. 31
   1. Renewed Strength
   2. Run and not weary
   3. Walk and not faint
