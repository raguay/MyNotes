1. Don't respect others - v. 1-9
	1. Giving more attention to someone rich than poor
		1. Even though the rich then often persecuted the Christians
	2. It is a sin!
		1. Because it's not Godlike.
		2. We need to be like God and not respect others based on their money and influences
2. Breaking one law breaks them all - v. 10-13
	1. How many laws do you have to break to be a transgressor? Just one.
	2. God brings this truth even closer.
		1. One law broken makes us guilty of every law!
		2. It's like a chain that each law is a link.
			1. One link breaks and we fall in hell!
			2. One law is all it takes, but we have ignored and broken so many more!
	3. The basis of breaking the law is simply rejecting God's authority in our lives.
		1. People want freedom from the law to do whatever they want.
		2. Many believe that getting saved is the way to get out of the law.
			1. It isn't. The law still applies. Just the ultimate punishment has been paid.
			2. God will still punish us in this life.
		3. The heart issue is still there: do you want to follow God?
			1. Someone that never wants to follow after Jesus has never been saved!
			2. The rest of the chapter explains this truth even more.
3. Faith without works - v. 14-26
	1. Faith causes a change in our actions. v. 14-20
		1. True faith means we are going from a self-centered life to a God-centered life.
		2. The works that true faith creates is a life that follows God
			1. The essence of faith is to love God and love others.
			2. Love always is demonstrated in our actions. 
	2. Abraham believed God - v. 21-24
		1. That he went to sacrifice Isaac
		2. He believed that God would raise him from the dead so that he still had grandchildren
			1. God promised that Isaac's children would inherit the land.
	3. Rahab protected the spies - v. 25-26
		1. Believing that their God would help her and her family.
		2. She believed that he is the only God and the only one that can save her.
		3. Her belief moved her to help God's people.
