1. Introduction - v. 1-2
   1. Standard greeting for that time period.
   2. Elect to be conformed to Christ's image
      1. Not elected for salvation
      2. Salvation is for anyone who believes.
      3. After salvation, God determines to sanctify us in the Spirit.
         1. That causes an outward change as well.
2. Blessing God for His salvation - v. 3-5
	1. We are saved by faith alone
	2.  God's power keeps us on the right track.
	3.  Anyone who trusts is Jesus is guaranteed a place in heaven with Him. 
3. Temptations of Life - v. 6-9
	1. Rejoicing even though currently having problems
		1. The Christian church was being heavily persecuted
		2. The persecution causes a heavy heart - depression
	2. But, they kept looking to Jesus whom they can't see
		1. It seems to be confusing - looking at what can't be seen
		2. It is an act of faith that knows that which they can't see is real and helping them.
	3. That type of faith is what saves them.
4.  prophets of old looked for - v. 10-12
	1. All the prophets of old wanted to understand when Christ would come.
	2. God did let they know was to help us understand God's plans.
5. Faith and holiness - v. 13-16 -stopped
	1. God expects us to walk with Him daily.
	2. Walking with Him, we will be holy.
	3. Otherwise, we will sin because we are walking in self will.
6. Our salvation - v. 17-23
	1. Realize what it costs God to redeem us
		1. Jesus had to leave heaven
		2. He had to be beaten and crucified
		3. He had to die and be buried for three days

	2. We are saved by God's blood
		1. Not by our works or money
		2. Our works and money will fade away.
		3. God's word will never fade away.
7. Our lives are for a short time, but not God's Word - v. 24-25
	1. Our life is short and fleeting
	2. The Lord and His salvation never fades away.
	3. That is what they learned from the Apostles.
