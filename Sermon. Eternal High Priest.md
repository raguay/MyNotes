Hebrews 7:19-28

1. Perfection
    1. Man can't achieve it
        1. No one has ever followed the law without ever messing up.
            - Romans 3:23 "For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God;"
            - โรม 3:23 "เหตุว่าทุกคนทำบาป และเสื่อมจากสง่าราศีของพระเจ้า"
        2. We are sinners that can't do good by ourselves
    2. Even though we try to keep the law, the law can't save anyone!
2. Priest of the tabernacle
    1. are but men
        1. Can't be made perfect by actions
        2. born in the state of a sinner
    2. Made priest by a law
        1. Just the line of Aaron
        2. No one else allowed except for God interceding
    3. They had to offer sacrifices often
        1. Because it did not remove the guilt of sin
        2. Nor the ultimate penalty of sin
    4. Therefore, they still died and others had to take their place
        1. They could not continue in their office of priest
        2. Death stopped their service for God
            1. Because everyone is a sinner and the wages of sin is Death
                - Romans 6:23 'For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord.'
                - โรม 6:23 "เพราะว่าค่าจ้างของความบาปคือความตาย แต่ของประทานของพระเจ้าคือชีวิตนิรันดร์ในพระเยซูคริสต์องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้าของเรา"
3. But Jesus
    1. Is the son of God
    2. Made a priest by an oath of God
    3. Offered a sacrifice of himself but once
        1. By allowing men to hang him on the Cross
        2. God curses those hanged on a tree
            - Galatians 3:13 'Christ hath redeemed us from the curse of the law, being made a curse for us: for it is written, Cursed is every one that hangeth on a tree:'
            - กาลาเทีย 3:13 "พระคริสต์ทรงไถ่เราให้พ้นความสาปแช่งแห่งพระราชบัญญัติ โดยการที่พระองค์ทรงยอมถูกสาปแช่งเพื่อเรา เพราะมีคำเขียนไว้ว่า ‘ทุกคนที่ต้องถูกแขวนไว้บนต้นไม้ก็ต้องถูกสาปแช่ง’"
                1. Jesus was cursed on the cross by receiving the sins of all mankind!
                2. But, death could not contain or control him
                    1. He rose again from the dead on the third day
    4. He ever lives to make intercession
        1. He can therefore save anyone forever!!!
